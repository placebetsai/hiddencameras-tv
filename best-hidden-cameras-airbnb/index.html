⚠️ Airbnb Camera Rules (2025)

  • ✅ Allowed: Outdoor cameras covering entrances, driveways, and exterior areas — must be disclosed in listing
  • ✅ Allowed: Indoor cameras in common areas (living room, kitchen) — must be disclosed and visible
  • ❌ Banned: Any camera in bedrooms, bathrooms, or sleeping areas
  • ❌ Banned: Any camera not disclosed in the listing description

How to Disclose Cameras to Guests

Airbnb requires hosts to list every camera in the "Safety devices" section of the listing and describe their location in the listing description. Example language:

"This property has one security camera in the living room facing the front door for guest safety. No cameras are in bedrooms or bathrooms."

Where Can You Place Cameras?

The safest placements that guests accept: front door interior (facing the door only), above the TV facing the room exit, or mounted near the entryway. Avoid pointing cameras toward seating areas or couches.

Do Guests Care?

Studies show 73% of guests are fine with disclosed cameras in common areas. The complaints come from undisclosed cameras — which can result in permanent account suspension and legal liability.
