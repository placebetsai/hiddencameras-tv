How to Detect Hidden Cameras
Before You Unpack

Airbnb confirmed 35,000+ hidden camera reports in 2025. Hotels, vacation rentals, and changing rooms are common targets. Here's exactly how to sweep any space in under 10 minutes.

Legal Reminder

If you find a hidden camera, photograph it in place, do not touch it, leave the property, and contact local police. Filing a report protects future guests. Airbnb's new 2026 policy mandates a full refund + relocation for confirmed cases.

The 6-Step Sweep (Do This Every Time)

01

Turn Off the Lights

In a dark room, use your phone flashlight at a low angle. Camera lenses reflect light distinctively — scan every object, especially those facing the bed, shower, or changing area.

02

Use Your Phone Camera

Some older phone front cameras (not blocked by IR filters) can see infrared light. Open your camera app, point a TV remote at it — if you see a purple glow, your front cam detects IR. Then scan the room for similar purple glows.

03

Scan with an RF Detector

Turn on an RF detector and slowly walk the room. Beeps spike near transmitting devices. Pay special attention to smoke detectors, clocks, and USB chargers. Wait 2 minutes — some cameras only transmit periodically.

04

Use a Lens Detector

In a dark room, hold the lens detector to your eye and scan slowly. Hidden camera lenses reflect the detector's infrared LEDs as a bright red dot — unlike anything else in the room.

05

Check the WiFi Network

Join the property's WiFi and use a network scanner app (Fing is free). Any device labeled "camera", "IPCam", or with an unknown MAC address is a red flag. Compare device count to what's visible.

06

Physical Inspection

Check smoke detectors, clocks, and USB chargers for tiny holes or mismatched design. Unplug or cover anything suspicious. Look for anything pointing directly at sleeping or bathing areas.

Where They Hide

These 8 locations account for 90% of discovered hidden cameras. Check them first.

🔥

Smoke Detectors

Most common hotel/Airbnb hiding spot. Look for asymmetric holes or a lens glint.

🕰️

Alarm Clocks & Radios

Especially digital clocks with tiny holes on the face or sides.

🔌

USB Charger Blocks

Spy cam chargers sold openly on Amazon. Look for an extra-thick charger cube.

📺

TV Bezels

Pinhole cameras inside the TV frame, aimed at the bed.

💡

Light Bulbs & Sockets

Full 360° WiFi cameras disguised as bulbs. Check unusual bulbs in bedrooms.

📚

Books & Decorations

Fake books, picture frames, stuffed animals — common in vacation rentals.

🌡️

Air Purifiers & Thermostats

Smart-looking devices that seem normal but have embedded lenses.

🪴

Plants & Vases

Tiny pinhole cameras embedded in artificial plants aimed at sofas or beds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can my iPhone detect hidden cameras?+

Partially. iPhone front cameras have IR filters so they won't see IR LEDs, but the regular camera sometimes can. Better: use a dedicated lens detector or RF detector. Free apps are mostly ineffective.

Do hidden cameras work without WiFi?+

Yes. Many spy cameras record locally to a microSD card and transmit nothing. RF detectors won't catch these. Only physical inspection and lens detectors will find them.

What do I do if I find a hidden camera in an Airbnb?+

Don't touch it. Photograph it in place showing context (what it's aimed at). Leave immediately. Report to Airbnb support (they now offer full refunds + relocation costs under 2026 policy). File a police report — recording someone in a private space without consent is a felony in most US states.

Are hidden camera detectors worth it?+

A $30 RF detector catches the vast majority of consumer-grade spy cams that transmit over WiFi or Bluetooth. Given that a single hidden camera incident can ruin a trip, it's cheap insurance. Serious travelers carry both an RF detector and a lens finder.

How common are hidden cameras in Airbnbs?+

Airbnb's own data shows tens of thousands of reports annually. Independent surveys suggest 1 in 12 guests find something suspicious on arrival. The risk is real and concentrated in short-term rentals, not hotels.
