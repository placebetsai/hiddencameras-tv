How to Install a Security Camera Without Drilling Any Holes

JB

Jordan Blake·March 27, 2026·4 min read

Why No-Drill Installation Matters

Whether you're renting an apartment, own a historic home, or simply want to avoid damaging walls, installing security cameras without drilling is increasingly practical. Modern wireless cameras eliminate the need for running cables through walls, and adhesive mounting solutions provide secure placement without permanent modifications. This approach protects your deposit, preserves your home's integrity, and allows flexibility to relocate cameras as needed.

Adhesive Mount Systems: The Foundation

Adhesive mounting represents the most straightforward no-drill solution for security cameras. These systems use industrial-strength double-sided tape or weatherproof adhesive pads designed to hold camera weight securely. The key to success lies in proper surface preparation and selecting the right adhesive strength for your camera's weight.

Preparing Your Mounting Surface

Before applying any adhesive mount, clean the target surface thoroughly with rubbing alcohol and allow it to dry completely. Dust, dirt, or moisture compromises adhesive bonding. For outdoor installations, choose smooth surfaces—rough stucco or textured brick reduces contact area and weakens the bond. Wipe the camera's mounting bracket with the same alcohol solution to remove manufacturing residue.

Temperature matters significantly. Adhesive bonds optimally between 50°F and 85°F. If installing during extreme temperatures, wait for conditions to normalize or use temperature-rated adhesive specifically formulated for your climate. Allow adhesive to cure fully—typically 24 to 48 hours—before mounting the camera.

Magnetic Mounting Solutions

Magnetic mounts offer exceptional flexibility for metal surfaces like gutters, downspouts, HVAC units, or metal window frames. These systems require no adhesive or tools, allowing quick repositioning to test camera angles before finalizing placement.

Choosing the Right Magnetic Mount

Select magnetic bases rated for weight significantly exceeding your camera's mass—a camera weighing 6 ounces should use a mount rated for 2+ pounds minimum. Magnetic strength diminishes with distance, so mounting brackets should position the magnet directly against the metal surface without spacers or paint interference.

Clean metal surfaces with a dry cloth before attaching magnetic mounts. Rust, paint, or coating reduces magnetic grip. For outdoor use, inspect magnetic mounts monthly, especially after weather events, to ensure they haven't shifted. The Blink Mini 2 — Best Budget → weighs only 4.2 ounces, making it ideal for magnetic mount installations.

Cord Management Without Drilling

Wireless cameras eliminate drilling for power cables, but managing cords from plug-in models requires alternatives to running cables through walls. Use adhesive cable clips to route power cords along baseboards, door frames, or exterior walls. These clips leave no permanent marks and come off cleanly.

For outdoor installations, run cables along gutters using gutter clips, or use adhesive-backed cable conduit to protect wires from weather while maintaining a no-drill approach. Ensure cables don't create trip hazards indoors or allow water pooling outdoors.

Corner Mounting and Ledge Placement

Interior corners and ledges offer natural camera positions without adhesive or magnetic mounting. Bookshelf placement, furniture top positioning, or window ledge installation works well for cameras with wide viewing angles. The Ring Indoor Cam 2nd Gen — Best Overall → includes a adjustable stand for precisely angled placement on any flat surface.

This approach suits temporary monitoring or situations requiring frequent adjustment. Ensure cameras sit far enough from edges to prevent accidental bumping or tipping. For permanent installations, this method provides less security than wall mounting.

Weatherproof Adhesive Considerations for Outdoor Use

Outdoor installations demand weatherproof adhesives rated for UV exposure, temperature fluctuation, and moisture. Standard interior double-sided tape fails within weeks outdoors. Look for adhesives specifically labeled for exterior use and tested in your climate zone.

  • Check adhesive temperature range against your annual high/low temperatures
  • Verify UV resistance ratings for sun-exposed locations
  • Confirm waterproofing credentials for rain-prone regions
  • Test adhesive on inconspicuous surfaces first

Final Recommendation

The Wyze Cam v4 — Best Value → combines wireless functionality with lightweight design, making it ideal for adhesive and magnetic mount installations. For most no-drill scenarios, prioritize adhesive mounting on smooth, clean surfaces indoors, and magnetic mounting on metal structures outdoors. Test camera angles with temporary positioning before applying permanent adhesives, and always allow adequate curing time. This measured approach ensures secure installation while preserving your home's condition and maintaining flexibility for future adjustments.
