How to Secure Your Front Door: Cameras + Smart Locks Guide

April 7, 2026·5 min read

Why Front Door Security Matters

Your front door is the primary entry point to your home. It's where packages arrive, guests visit, and unfortunately, where most burglaries begin. A comprehensive front door security strategy combines video surveillance with smart access control—giving you visibility of who's at your door and control over who enters.

The combination of a quality doorbell camera and smart lock creates redundant security layers. You'll know who approaches your home before they knock, have video proof of any incidents, and can grant or deny access remotely.

Choosing the Right Doorbell Camera

A doorbell camera serves as your front-line defense. It provides real-time alerts, video recording, and two-way communication with visitors—whether you're home or away.

Key Features to Prioritize

  • Resolution: Look for at least 1080p, with 2K or 4K preferred for identifying faces and license plates
  • Night Vision: Infrared or color night vision captures clear footage after dark
  • Field of View: 160-180 degrees covers your entire porch without blind spots
  • Video Storage: Local storage via microSD card or cloud backup ensures footage isn't lost
  • Two-Way Audio: Communicate with visitors or deter suspicious activity in real-time
  • Motion Detection: Customizable zones reduce false alerts from street traffic
  • Smart Home Integration: Works with your existing ecosystem (Alexa, Google Home, Apple HomeKit)

Top Doorbell Cameras Compared

Ring Indoor Cam Ring Indoor Cam → offers solid 1080p performance with reliable cloud storage and Alexa integration. It's affordable and works well for most homeowners, though it's technically designed for indoor use (though many use it at covered entryways).

Blink Mini 2 Blink Mini 2 → provides compact form factor with 1080p video and local storage option. Motion detection is responsive, making it ideal if you want minimal subscription costs.

Google Nest Cam Google Nest Cam → delivers 2K resolution with excellent night vision. If you're already in Google's ecosystem, its integration is seamless. However, it requires a Nest Aware subscription for recording longer than 3 hours.

Wyze Cam v4 Wyze Cam v4 → balances cost and performance with 1080p, color night vision, and person detection. It's one of the best budget options available.

Eufy S350 Eufy S350 → offers dual cameras for wide coverage, 4K resolution, and privacy-focused local storage. It's pricier but requires minimal cloud dependency.

Selecting a Smart Lock

A smart lock replaces or works alongside your traditional deadbolt, allowing remote access, keyless entry, and detailed access logs. Combined with a doorbell camera, it eliminates the need for physical keys and provides complete visibility and control.

Smart Lock Installation Considerations

  • Retrofit vs. Replace: Retrofit locks work over your existing deadbolt (easier installation). Replacement locks require removing your current lock (more involved but cleaner)
  • Power Source: Battery-powered locks are common and last 6-12 months. Hardwired options require electrical work but are maintenance-free
  • Connectivity: WiFi-enabled locks allow instant remote access. Bluetooth-only locks work from nearby but not remotely
  • Backup Access: Ensure the lock has multiple access methods: app control, keypad code, physical key, and fingerprint
  • Smart Home Compatibility: Verify it works with your existing system (Apple HomeKit, Google Home, or Alexa)

Smart Lock Options

Popular models include Level Lock (works inside existing deadbolts with minimal visibility), August Smart Lock Pro (retrofit style with good app control), Yale Assure Lock 2 Plus (reliable with HomeKit support), and U-Bolt Pro (affordable with strong keypad functionality).

Integration Strategy: Camera + Lock Working Together

The real power comes from using doorbell cameras and smart locks as a coordinated system.

Practical Workflows

  • Package Delivery: See the delivery driver, grant temporary lock access to a trusted person, monitor the handoff via video
  • Emergency Access: Share temporary codes with contractors or service people without being home—watch them arrive via camera
  • Security Verification: When motion is detected at your door, view the live feed before unlocking for someone claiming to be a visitor
  • Detailed Logging: Create an audit trail combining lock access logs with video footage to prove who accessed your home and when
  • Automated Responses: Set automations: when your camera detects motion between 10 PM and 6 AM, lock your door automatically if it isn't already

Installation and Setup

Doorbell Camera Installation: Most doorbell cameras require a 16-24V power source from existing wiring. If your home has a traditional wired doorbell, installation is straightforward—disconnect the old one and connect the new camera to the same terminals. Battery-powered models need no wiring but require regular charging.

Smart Lock Installation: This varies by model. Retrofit locks typically require removing your interior lock cover and sliding the new device over your existing deadbolt. Replacement locks require more extensive work. If uncomfortable with DIY installation, hire a locksmith ($100-200 labor).

Network Setup: Both devices need stable WiFi. Position your router centrally or use mesh WiFi to ensure strong signal at your front door. Test connectivity before finalizing installation.

Privacy and Security Best Practices

  • Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts
  • Use strong, unique passwords for each device's app account
  • Disable cloud storage if you prefer footage not synced to manufacturer servers; use local storage instead
  • Review privacy policies—understand what data companies collect and store
  • Keep firmware updated to patch security vulnerabilities
  • Limit app access permissions to necessary functions only

Final Recommendation

For most homeowners, pair a mid-range doorbell camera like the Google Nest Cam or Wyze Cam v4 with a WiFi-enabled smart lock such as Level Lock or Yale Assure Lock 2 Plus. This combination offers excellent visibility, remote access, and integration without excessive cost or complexity. If you want maximum privacy and minimal subscription fees, choose the Eufy S350 with a battery-powered smart lock. If you're already committed to Google's ecosystem, Nest Cam pairs seamlessly with Google Home and most smart locks. Test both devices on your network before permanent installation, and always maintain physical key access as a backup.
